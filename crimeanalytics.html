Theft of Motor Vehicle Contents or Parts (Car prowl) is the Most Common Type of Criminal Activity in the Summer in Both Seattle and San Francisco.¶

Moreover, the majority of car prowls take place between 3PM-1AM.

Top Ten Criminal Activities¶

To begin, we look at the top ten criminal activities (using Descript/Summarized Offense Description) in each city. From the bar charts below, we see that the most common crime in Seattle is Theft-Carprowl, and that in San Francisco is Grand Theft from Locked Auto. Also, we color the bars based on Category/Offense Type, from which we see the highest occurring crime in Seattle is in the Car Prowl category, and that in San Francisco is in Larceny/Theft, which is the equivalent of Car Prowl as infered from the descriptions of the actual criminal activities.

# Read in the columns that we will use in our data analysis
sf_fields = ['Category', 'Descript', 'Date', 'Time', 'Location']
sea_fields = ['Summarized Offense Description', 'Offense Type', 'Occurred Date or Date Range Start', 'Location']

df_sf = pd.read_csv('sanfrancisco_incidents_summer_2014.csv', usecols=sf_fields)

# relabel Seattle data to match the labels used for San Francisco
df_sea.columns = ['Descript', 'Category', 'Date', 'Location']

# replace "Date" and "Time" with into a new column "Timestamp" of type <datetime>
format_sea_time = lambda x: datetime.strptime(x, "%m/%d/%Y %I:%M:%S %p")
df_sea['Timestamp'] = pd.Series(df_sea['Date'].map(format_sea_time))
df_sea = df_sea.drop('Date', 1)

format_sf_date = lambda x: datetime.strptime(x, "%m/%d/%Y %H:%M")
format_sf_time = lambda x: datetime.strptime(x, "%H:%M")
df_sf['Timestamp'] = pd.Series((df_sf['Date']+' '+df_sf['Time']).map(format_sf_date))
df_sf = df_sf.drop('Date', 1)
df_sf = df_sf.drop('Time', 1)

sea_crime_count = OrderedDict(sorted(Counter(df_sea['Descript']).items(), key=lambda t: t[1], reverse=True))
sf_crime_count = OrderedDict(sorted(Counter(df_sf['Descript']).items(), key=lambda t: t[1], reverse=True))

sea_cat_desc_dict = dict(zip(df_sea['Descript'], df_sea['Category']))
sf_cat_desc_dict = dict(zip(df_sf['Descript'], df_sf['Category']))

# Setup colors for different categories
cmap = plt.get_cmap('rainbow')

# Uniqify list
def uniquify(seq): 
    # order preserving
    noDupes = []
    [noDupes.append(i) for i in seq if not noDupes.count(i)]
    return noDupes

sea_top_crimes = uniquify([sea_cat_desc_dict[v] for v in sea_crime_count.keys()[:10]])
sf_top_crimes = uniquify([sf_cat_desc_dict[v] for v in sf_crime_count.keys()[:10]])
sea_colors = ['maroon', 'red', 'darkorange', 'tan', 'darkblue', 'rosybrown', 'g', 'salmon', 'orangered']
sf_colors = ['maroon', 'red', 'burlywood', 'skyblue', 'darkblue', 'yellowgreen']
sea_d2c = OrderedDict(zip(sea_top_crimes, sea_colors))
sf_d2c = OrderedDict(zip(sf_top_crimes, sf_colors))

def desc2color(cat_desc_dict, d2c, desc):
    return d2c[cat_desc_dict[desc]]

sea_top_colors = [desc2color(sea_cat_desc_dict, sea_d2c, x) for x in sea_crime_count.keys()[:10]]
sf_top_colors = [desc2color(sf_cat_desc_dict, sf_d2c, x) for x in sf_crime_count.keys()[:10]]

    for rect in rects:
        height = rect.get_height()
        ax.text(rect.get_x() + rect.get_width()/2., height, '%d' % int(height), ha='center', va='bottom', fontsize=13)

Top Criminal Activities by Month¶

We also observed an interesting trend that the number of crime activities has decreased monthly in Seattle but has increased every month in San Francisco.

Daily Criminal Activity Trends¶

Finally, we look at how the number of criminal activities change over the day. In both cities, the hours between 3AM-5:30AM show the lowest rate of criminal activities, while the hours between 8PM-11PM show the highest rate of crimianl activities. Also, there is spike in criminal activity around noon time in both cities. The trends also holds on a categorical basis with the except of the categories Burglary, Other Property, and Shoplifting in Seattle, which are more frequent earlier in the day (between 7AM-12PM).
